About NeverSleep

This project prevents your device screen from automatically sleeping. It includes draggable widgets for Clock, To-Do, Pomodoro timing, and Battery status, with position and visibility settings saved locally.

This project is based on keep-awake by CarolinaMoraes, which itself was inspired by nosleep.page.

Troubleshooting Tip: Since all your settings, tasks, and widget positions are saved locally in your browser, simply refreshing the page is usually a safe first step if you encounter any display issues or bugs. Your data won't be lost on refresh.

Why NeverSleep?

Whether you're monitoring a live dashboard, following a recipe hands-free, reading a lengthy article, or giving a presentation — your screen dimming at the wrong moment is always frustrating. NeverSleep solves this with a single click, using the modern Screen Wake Lock API (with smart fallbacks for older browsers) to keep your display active for as long as you need it.

On top of that, NeverSleep comes packed with handy productivity widgets — a live clock, a to-do list, a Pomodoro timer, and a battery monitor — all draggable and customizable to fit your workflow. Your settings, tasks, and widget positions are stored locally in your browser, so everything is right where you left it every time you come back.

Troubleshooting & Help
Having Issues? Try Refreshing!

Sometimes visual glitches or minor bugs might occur (like a widget not appearing correctly after changing modes).

Because all your settings (like widget visibility/position, clock format) and data (To-Do list items, Pomodoro state) are stored directly in your browser's local storage, it is safe to simply refresh the page (F5 or Cmd/Ctrl+R).

Refreshing will reload the application using your saved data, often resolving temporary display problems without any loss of information.
